I. Chronology

1869: Nuclein

Friedrich Miescher isolates DNA. High acidic phosphorus content detected in the nuclei.

1952: Photo 51

Franklin & Wilkins provide the X-Ray Diffraction data, revealing the periodicity of 34Å and 3.4Å.

1953: The Model

Watson and Crick propose the right-handed B-DNA structure based on spatial stoichiometry.

II. Metric Parameters

Pitch / Turn

34 Å per 10 base pairs. Approximately 10.4-10.5 bp/turn in solution.

Helix Diameter

Constant 20 Å (2nm) ensured by Purine-Pyrimidine strict bonding parity.

Bond Lengths

C-1' to N linkage: 1.47 Å. Major groove: 22 Å. Minor groove: 12 Å.

III. Applied Math

L = Tw + Wr

Linking Number: A biostatistical constant.
Twist (local helix winding) + Writhe (global coiling).

Chargaff Logic

[A] + [G] = [T] + [C]. Ratios of Purines:Pyrimidines converge at 1.00.

Hyperchromicity

Absorbance at 260nm increases (30-40%) during melting due to stacking loss.

IV. Thermodynamics

Stacking Interaction

Primary stabilizer: π-π interaction between planar nitrogenous bases. Stacking ΔH ≈ -10 kcal/mol.

Torsion Angles

Conformation is driven by 7 torsion angles per residue, primarily Alpha to Zeta and the Chi bond.

Sugar Pucker

C2'-endo vs C3'-endo. In B-DNA, the 2' carbon is on the 'endo' side, shaping the major groove.
